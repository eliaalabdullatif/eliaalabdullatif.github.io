Projects

Northeastern University · Jan 2026 – Apr 2026

Segway Vehicle Control System Design

Designed and tuned controllers to stabilize an inverted-pendulum Segway model in MATLAB and Simulink, achieving a 1-second settling time under simulated rider-lean disturbances.

  • Derived Laplace and state-space representations and verified controllability analytically and numerically
  • Implemented PID, full-state feedback, and reduced-order pole-placement controllers
  • Identified the reduced-order controller as most efficient: ~18× less peak torque and ~14× less peak power than full-state feedback
  • Built Luenberger observers to estimate unmeasured states with near-perfect reconstruction
MATLAB Simulink Control Systems State-Space
Toyota Motor North America · Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

Engine Hood Vibration Analysis

Capstone collaboration with Toyota R&D investigating how hood thickness affects flutter and stiffness performance.

  • Performed stiffness testing using a FANUC robotic arm with a load cell, applying 100 N at 8 discrete locations across two hood variants
  • Instrumented hoods with a 4-channel accelerometer array and applied FFT signal processing to identify resonance frequencies
  • Showed a 10% reduction in hood thickness caused a 32% drop in stiffness, recommending the standard hood design to Toyota R&D
Vibration Analysis FFT FANUC Structural Testing
Michigan State University · Oct 2023 – Dec 2023

Horizontal Motion Cereal Shaker

Designed, fabricated, and prototyped a mechanized cereal transport system using a cam-follower drive and elastic spring elements for vibration control.

  • Built a steel shaker bed with industrial-grade rollers and a custom cam-follower mechanism for repeatable horizontal motion
  • Integrated bungee cords as elastic spring elements to smooth material transfer
  • Placed 2nd out of 15 teams in cereal transfer rate efficiency
