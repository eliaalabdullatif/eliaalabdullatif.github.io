SOLIDWORKS · 2026

Quadcopter Frame Design & Structural Analysis

Designed a quadcopter frame from scratch in SOLIDWORKS, featuring a 70×70mm central plate with four spider arms supporting motor mounts at the corners. The load-bearing spider arm was engineered for additive manufacturing using Nylon 101, with a triangulated cutout pattern for weight reduction and a raised motor mount tower at the tip. The arm was validated against the two ways a structural component can fail in service: static loading and dynamic resonance. The static study applied a 20 N peak thrust load to the motor mount platform with the frame mounting bolts held fixed. Peak von Mises stress reached 25 MPa at the motor mount transition, giving a factor of safety of 2.4 against the material's 60 MPa yield strength. The stress concentration at this transition identifies the location to reinforce if the design is scaled up to higher-thrust motors. Modal analysis identified the first two natural frequencies at 188 Hz (vertical bending) and 247 Hz (lateral bending). These modes establish the frequency bands that propeller excitation must avoid to prevent resonant amplification. At typical multirotor cruise and hover speeds, propeller blade excitation lies well above both modes, placing the arm in the attenuation region of its transmissibility curve, where the structure passively filters high-frequency motor vibration before it reaches the flight controller.

Segway Vehicle Control System Design

Modeled and stabilized an inverted-pendulum Segway in MATLAB and Simulink using PID, full-state feedback, reduced-order pole placement, and Luenberger observers. The reduced-order controller required 18× less peak torque and 14× less peak power than full-state feedback while maintaining stability.

SOLIDWORKS · 2025

Articulated Robotic Arm with Parallel-Jaw Gripper

Articulated 2-DOF arm with a custom parallel-jaw end effector, modeled as a nested sub-assembly. The base actuator drives the platform's yaw rotation from below, while the shoulder actuator on top controls pitch, together positioning the gripper across the arm's reachable workspace. The gripper's design challenge: keep the jaw faces parallel through full open/close travel without using a linear rail. Solved with a four-bar linkage where the coupler link carries the jaw face, chosen for lower part count and easier manufacturing than a rack-and-pinion alternative. Synchronized pulleys drive both linkages from a single actuator, ensuring symmetric closure.

Michigan State University · 2023

Horizontal Motion Cereal Shaker

Team design competition: build a mechanized system that transfers cereal across a horizontal bed at maximum throughput. Solved with a cam-follower drive translating rotary input into precise, repeatable linear oscillation of a steel shaker bed mounted on industrial rollers. Bungee cords integrated as tunable elastic elements to dampen unwanted vibration and smooth material transfer. Placed 2nd out of 15 teams in transfer rate efficiency.
