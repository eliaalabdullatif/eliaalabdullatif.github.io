SOLIDWORKS · 2024 – Present

Three-Arm Bearing Puller

Three-arm bearing puller modeled as a fully-mated SOLIDWORKS assembly with self-centering geometry: a single driving dimension keeps all three arms symmetric about the screw axis, so the tool grips concentrically at any opening width. A threaded mate on the central drive screw produces realistic linear travel under rotation. Built with manufacturing intent: standard fastener sizes, proper clearances at pivot joints, and assembly-ready mating surfaces throughout.

SOLIDWORKS · 2024 – Present

Articulated Robotic Arm with Parallel-Jaw Gripper

Articulated arm with a custom parallel-jaw end effector modeled as a nested sub-assembly. The gripper's design challenge: keep the jaw faces parallel through full open/close travel without using a linear rail. Solved with a four-bar linkage where the coupler link carries the jaw face, chosen for lower part count and easier manufacturing than a rack-and-pinion alternative. Synchronized pulleys drive both linkages from a single actuator, ensuring symmetric closure.
